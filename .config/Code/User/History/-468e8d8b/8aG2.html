Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Robotics

AI refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions. AI is generally associated with computer science, but it has many important links with other fields such as maths, psychology, linguistics, cognition, biology, neuroscience, etc. AI is the study of how to make computers do things that, at the moment, people do better.

Applications of AI

  • Healthcare Industry

    • AI is being tested and used in the healthcare industry for dosing drugs and idfferent treatment in patients, and for surgical procedures.

  • Autonomous driving

    • Machines must weigh the consequences of any action as each action will impact the result. For autonomous cars, the computer system must account for all external data and compute it to act in a way that prevents collisions.

  • Playing game

    • General game playing (GGP) and General video game playing (GVGP)is the concept and designs of AI. For instance, in the GGP of chess, computers are programmed to play these games using a specially designed algorithm. It is considered a necessary landmark on the way to AI.

  • Computer vision

    • It is a file of AI, which enables the computer and its systems to get input in the form of digital images and videos and take action based on the provided input.

  • Finance industry

    • AI is used to detect and flag activity in banking and finance such as unusual debit card usage and large account deposits which help a bank's fraud department.

Robotics

It is a branch of engineering, that invloves the conception, design, manufacture, and operation of robots which overlaps with electronics, computer science, artificial intelligence, mechatronics and bioengineering. Sir Isaax Asimov is often given credit for being the first person to use the term robotics who composed three principles in the 1940s about the behavior of robots. Those are;

  1. Robots must never harm human beings
  2. Robots must follow instructions from humans without violating rule 1
  3. Robots must protect themselves without violating the other rules.

Cloud Computing

CLoud computing uses various services, such as software development platforms, servers, storage, and software over the Internet, often referred to as a cloud.

Types of Cloud

Clouds can be classified as public, private, and hybrid.
Public cloud is made available to the general public.
Private cloud computing environment resides within the boundaries of an organization and is used exclusively for organizational benefits.
Hybrid cloud is the combination of both public and private cloud.

Advantages of Cloud Computing

  1. Cost

    2. It is probably the most efficient method to use, maintain and upgrade.

  2. Unlimited storage

    3. Storing information in the cloud gives us almost unlimited storage capacity

  3. Backup and recovery

    4. Since, all the data is stored in the cloud, backing it up and restoring is relatively much easier than storing on a physical device

  4. Quick deployment

    5. Once the method of functioning is selected, the entire system can be fully functional in a matter of few minutes

  5. Security

    6. Many cloud providers offer a broad set of policies, technologies, and controls that strengthen the overall security, helping protect your data, apps and infrastructure from potential threats.

Big Data

Big Data is a collection of data that is huge in volume, yet growing exponentially with time.

Types of Big Data

  1. Structured

    2. Any data which can be stored, accessed and processed in the form of fixed format is termed as a structured data.

  2. Unstructured

    3. Any data with unknown form or the structure is classified as unstructured data. This makes it very difficult and time-consuming to process and analyse teh unstructured data.

  3. Semi-structured

    4. This data contain both the forms of data. We can see semi-structured data as a structured in form but it is actually not defined with e.g., a table definition in relational DBMS. Example: XML file.

Characteristics of Big Data

  1. Variety

    2. It refers to the variety of data gathered from multiple sources.

  2. Velocity

    3. It refers to the speed at which data is being created in real-time. It also comprises the rate of change, linking of incoming data sets at varying speeds, and activity bursts.

  3. Volume

    4. Big Data indicates huge 'volumes' of data that are being generated daily from various sources like social media platforms, business processes, machines, networks, human interactions, etc.

  4. Veracity

    5. It refers to the reliability or trustworthiness of the data. Due to the large volume of data, we have uncertainity about the validity, the accurateness of data.

  5. Value

    6. It refers to the worth of business value of the collected data

  6. Variability

    7. It refers to the inconsistency of big data and how the big data can be used and formatted.

Virtual Reality

Virtual reality (VR) is a term that expresses computer-based simulated environments that can perceive as the real world and the unreal world. It is a computer-simulated or artificial environment created with software and presented to the user so that the user believes and accepts it as a real environment.

Appplications of VR

  • It can be used in entertainment like games and movies to make the gaming experience more real and to allow individuals to experience adventures under extreme conditions.
  • Virtual surgical models are used to give information to the doctors regarding the response of various organs towards the various surgical instruments used suring complex surgery.
  • VR is also used in training. It is used to train people, for automobile driving.

E-Commerce

E-commerce stands for electronic commerce and is generally described as a method of buying and selling products and services electronically through internet.

Classification of e-Commerce

Types Description
B2B (Business to Business) Sells products or services to other businesses. e.g., www.freemarkets.com
B2C (Business to Consumer) Sells products or services directly to consumers. e.g., www.amazon.com, www.yahoo.com
C2B (Consumer to Business) Consumer fixes a price on their own, which businesses accept or decline. e.g., www.priceline.com
C2C (Consumer to Comsumer) Consumer sells directly to other consumer. e.g., www.ebay.com

Advantages of e-Commerce

  • Expands the company's market place to national and international markets
  • Enables the companies to procure material and services from other companies rapidly and at less cost
  • Gives the consumers more choices in selecting products and vendors
  • It facilitates competition, as a result of substantial didcounts.
  • Enables consumers to shop or makee other transactions 24 hours a day, from any location

    • Disadvantages of e-Commerce

    • Lack of universal for standard, security and reliability
    • Many companies have had trouble recruiting and retaining employees with the technological, design, and business process skills needed to create an effective e-commerce presence.
    • Many businesses face cultural and legal barrier to e-commerce

    Internet of Things (IoT)

    Connection of billions of devices like electronics, sensors, gateways, and actuators over a wireless network can be referred to as IoT. Connecting up all different objects and adding sensors adds a level of digital intelligence to devices that would be otherwise dumb, enabling them to communicate real-time data without involving a human being.
    Driverless trucks, auto-pilot airplanes, and a light bulb with a smartphone sensor are a few examples of IoT.

    Advantages of IoT

    • It automates tasks and helps to improve the quality of a business's services and reduces the need for human intervention
    • Helps to operate business operations more efficiently, better understand customers to deliver enhanced customer service
    • Provides improved communication between connected electronic devices
    • Transferring data packets over a connected network saves time, effort, and money
    • It has the ability to access information from anywhere at any time on any device

    Disadvantages of IoT

    • As the number of connected devices increases and more information is shared between devices, the chances of the system being attacked also increases
    • Organizations may eventually have to deal with massive number of IoT devices, and collecting and managing the data from all those devices will be challenging
    • If there's a bug in the system, every connected device will likely become corrupted
    • Since there's no international standard of compatibility for IoT, it's difficult for devices from different manufactures to communicate with each other.
